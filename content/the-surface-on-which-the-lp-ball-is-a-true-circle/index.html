the surface on which the Lp ball is a true circle §

the L2 circle is the equidistant locus from a point on a flat plane. what surface makes the L1 diamond, the L∞ square, and the intermediate Lp shapes equidistant?

each radial ray on the surface has length equal to the Lp-norm of its xy-projection. the red loop on the surface is the geodesic circle of radius 1; its shadow on the floor is the yellow Lp ball.

for p<2 the surface arches up along the axes and dips down along the diagonals — the shadow squeezes inward. as p drops below 1 the diagonals plunge so steeply that the shadow becomes a non-convex four-pointed star (an astroid in the limit), collapsing toward the bare coordinate axes as p→0. for p>2 the surface flips: ridges along the diagonals, valleys along the axes, and the shadow stretches outward into a square as p→∞. at p=2 the surface is the flat plane.
