about how i think about what works in software.
> this one is about software reproducibility. 
---

imagine this:

you are onboarded as a contractor/advisor/employee/member of technical
staff..., at your local software mill.

you were hired because a friend of yours, now your coworker, was desparate:
none of the software this company builds seems to work 100% of the time.

> yea man, i just have a couple of bugs i just can't seem to figure out
but like, it runs most of the time, it's just that our biggest customer
sometimes runs into these super weird edge cases.

great friend (and hopefully enterprising individual) that you are, you took him
up on the offer.

however, on your first day, you come to the rapid realization on the true
gravity of the situation.

it's not __just__ that there are some bugs in the software, but moreso that no
one person seems to know precisely what is going on in the entire codebase.
